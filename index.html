About the Premier League

History

The Premier League was founded in 1992 when clubs in the Football League First Division broke away from the English Football League to take advantage of a lucrative television rights deal. Since then, it has grown to become the most-watched football league in the world, broadcast in 212 territories to 643 million homes and a potential TV audience of 4.7 billion people.

Competition Format

The Premier League operates on a system of promotion and relegation with the English Football League (EFL). The season runs from August to May, with each team playing 38 matches (playing each other home and away). Three points are awarded for a win, one point for a draw, and none for a loss. The teams are ranked by total points, then goal difference, and then goals scored.

Records & Achievements

  • Most titles: Manchester United (13)
  • Most points in a season: Manchester City (100 in 2017-18)
  • Most goals in a season: Erling Haaland (36 in 2022-23)
  • Longest unbeaten run: Arsenal (49 games from May 2003 to October 2004)
  • Fastest goal: Shane Long (7.69 seconds for Southampton vs Watford in 2019)

Global Impact

The Premier League is the most-watched sports league in the world. In the 2018-19 season, the cumulative attendance across all matches was 14,508,981, the highest of any association football league. The league's global appeal is demonstrated by its international fanbase, with matches broadcast to over 200 countries and territories.

Current Season Highlights (2024/25)

The 2024-25 season is the 33rd season of the Premier League. Liverpool are the defending champions, having won their 20th English league title the previous season. This season sees the introduction of new technologies like semi-automated offside calls and continued efforts to improve fan engagement through digital platforms.
